**1. Economic Scale, Growth Trajectory & Forward Momentum**
The economy exhibits a mature but stabilizing growth profile, with GDP at current prices reflecting steady expansion over the past decade (5-year CAGR: ~2.8%) and projected stabilization in the next five years (CAGR: ~2.5%). GDP per capita growth aligns with this trend, suggesting moderate but consistent purchasing power expansion. While cyclical rebounds post-pandemic are evident, the underlying trajectory indicates a structurally constrained macro landscape rather than an accelerating high-potential market. Retail expansion confidence should prioritize defensive positioning, targeting resilient segments within a mature consumption base rather than aggressive scaling assumptions.

**2. Stability, Inflation & Cyclical Risk Profile**
Unemployment trends (5-year CAGR: -1.2%) and youth unemployment (CAGR: -2.08%) signal labor market normalization, though elevated youth joblessness (20.2% in 2025) introduces medium-term demand fragility. Inflation (HICP) has moderated, with forward projections indicating stabilization, but consumer confidence remains flat (latest: 100.052), reflecting cautious discretionary spending sentiment. The environment is consumption-supportive but cyclically exposed, requiring pricing power discipline and margin resilience strategies to mitigate short-term revenue volatility.

**3. Income Structure & Luxury Market Depth**
Income distribution reveals a polarized structure, with the top 10% holding 26.9% of income (CAGR: +0.22%) and the bottom 10% at 2.7% (CAGR: -0.72%). The widening spread (24.2%) suggests premium-segment concentration rather than broad middle-class depth. GDP per capita, when coupled with this inequality, indicates limited broad-based aspirational demand, favoring high-end luxury concentration. Assortment positioning should prioritize exclusive, high-margin offerings, while long-term brand scaling must navigate structurally constrained discretionary upside.
