**1. Economic Scale, Growth Trajectory & Forward Momentum**
The economy exhibits a mature but stable consumption base, with GDP growth stabilizing around 2.1% (5-year CAGR) and projected to remain flat over the next five years. GDP per capita has grown at a 1.8% CAGR, reflecting moderate purchasing power expansion. While structural growth trends are decelerating, they are not collapsing, suggesting a resilient but unaccelerating market. The absence of cyclical rebounds indicates a steady-state environment rather than post-shock normalization. For retail expansion, this implies medium-term confidence in steady demand but limited upside from rapid economic acceleration. Expansion strategies should prioritize efficiency over aggressive scaling.

**2. Stability, Inflation & Cyclical Risk Profile**
Unemployment trends are stable, with youth unemployment declining at a 1.52% CAGR, reducing generational demand pressure. Inflation is projected to stabilize, mitigating real income erosion, while consumer confidence remains flat (98.242 in 2025-12). The labor market shows no signs of fragility, and inflation dynamics support stable real consumption growth. However, the lack of confidence improvement suggests cautious discretionary spending. Retail pricing power and margin resilience are likely to hold, but revenue visibility may be constrained by subdued sentiment. The environment is consumption-supportive but not expansionary.

**3. Income Structure & Luxury Market Depth**
Income distribution reveals a polarized structure, with the top 10% holding 25.0% of income (CAGR -1.31%) and the bottom 10% holding 3.0% (CAGR -1.28%). The narrowing spread suggests a slight reduction in inequality, but the concentration remains high. GDP per capita of $35,200 (2025) aligns with a premium-segment concentration rather than broad middle-class depth. The income architecture structurally favors high-end luxury capture over mid-market expansion. Assortment positioning should prioritize premium offerings, with price architecture emphasizing exclusivity. Long-term brand scaling should target affluent demographics rather than mass-market penetration.
