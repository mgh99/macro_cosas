**1. Economic Scale, Growth Trajectory & Forward Momentum**
The economy exhibits a structurally constrained macro landscape, with GDP growth in constant prices decelerating over the past five years and projected to remain subdued in the next five. While GDP at current prices has expanded, this reflects nominal rather than real growth, suggesting inflationary rather than productivity-driven gains. GDP per capita, a critical proxy for purchasing power depth, shows modest gains but lacks acceleration, indicating limited structural expansion of the middle-class consumer base. The absence of accelerating growth trends suggests retail expansion should prioritize defensive positioning over aggressive scaling, with a focus on margin resilience over volume-driven strategies.

**2. Stability, Inflation & Cyclical Risk Profile**
The labor market presents cyclical fragility, with youth unemployment hovering near 54% and showing only marginal improvement (CAGR -1.51%). This structural weakness signals generational demand pressure and constrained discretionary spending power among younger cohorts. Inflation, as measured by the Harmonised Index of Consumer Prices, has been volatile but is projected to stabilize, mitigating real income erosion. However, consumer confidence trends remain muted, reflecting cautious spending sentiment. The environment thus favors pricing discipline and inventory optimization over aggressive promotional strategies, with short-term revenue visibility dependent on cost management rather than demand elasticity.

**3. Income Structure & Luxury Market Depth**
The income distribution is highly concentrated, with the top 10% holding 42.7% of income while the bottom 10% retains just 1.4%, creating a polarized luxury niche. This structure favors high-end segmentation over broad-based retail, as aspirational consumption is concentrated among a narrow elite. GDP per capita, when adjusted for inequality, further limits effective purchasing power for premium goods. Retail strategies should emphasize exclusivity and premium pricing, with assortment positioning skewed toward ultra-luxury rather than mid-market expansion. Long-term brand scaling will require targeted high-net-worth customer acquisition rather than mass-market penetration.
