**1. Economic Scale, Growth Trajectory & Forward Momentum**
The economy exhibits a mature but stable consumption base, with GDP growth stabilizing around 2.1% CAGR over the past five years and projected to maintain this trajectory over the next five years. GDP per capita has grown at a 1.8% CAGR, indicating moderate purchasing power expansion. While structural growth is not accelerating, it is also not decelerating, suggesting a resilient but constrained macro landscape. The absence of cyclical rebounds in GDP per capita signals steady, if unremarkable, expansion. For retail, this implies medium-term confidence in expansion but with limited upside from rapid economic acceleration. Luxury and premium segments may face slower demand growth unless supported by targeted income strategies.

**2. Stability, Inflation & Cyclical Risk Profile**
Unemployment trends are improving, with youth unemployment declining at an 8.4% CAGR, reducing generational demand pressure. However, consumer confidence remains flat near 99.8, signaling cautious discretionary spending. Inflation projections show stabilization, mitigating real income erosion risks. The labor market’s gradual tightening suggests stable but not robust consumption growth. The environment is consumption-supportive but lacks cyclical fragility, implying steady revenue visibility. Retail pricing power may be constrained by flat confidence, while margins could benefit from stable inflation. Short-term revenue visibility is moderate, favoring defensive positioning over aggressive expansion.

**3. Income Structure & Luxury Market Depth**
Income distribution trends reveal a narrowing but still pronounced gap, with the top 10% holding 24.5% of income (down 0.6% CAGR) and the bottom 10% holding 2.2% (up 0.9% CAGR). This suggests a polarized structure where premium segments retain depth, but broad middle-class expansion is limited. GDP per capita of $38,200 (2025) supports aspirational consumption but not mass-market luxury penetration. The income architecture favors a high-end luxury concentration strategy, with niche premium capture potential. Assortment positioning should prioritize exclusivity over volume, and price architecture must align with top-tier affordability thresholds. Long-term brand scaling will require targeted high-income segmentation.
