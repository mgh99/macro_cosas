**Structural Consumer Base & Demographic Momentum**
The market exhibits structurally declining demographic fundamentals, with total population projected to shrink by 6.3% over the next decade, driven by persistent natural decline (average annual deficit of -280k) and volatile but insufficient migration inflows. The core consumption cohort (25–49) is contracting at a 1.2% CAGR, while the working-age population (15–64) is projected to shrink by 5.1%, signaling eroding demand capacity. Ageing dynamics are accelerating, with the 65+ cohort growing by 1.8% annually and the 80+ segment expanding by 2.1%, intensifying dependency pressures. Migration partially offsets natural decline but remains insufficient to sustain momentum, creating a structurally fragile equilibrium. This implies long-term retail contraction, labor shortages, and demand fragility, necessitating strategic consolidation in high-value segments and operational efficiency to offset shrinking addressable markets.

**Household Structure & Consumption Architecture**
The market is transitioning toward individualized consumption, with stable household sizes (2.4) masking structural shifts toward smaller, older households. The ageing population combined with shrinking core cohorts suggests a rebalancing toward premium, discretionary spending rather than broad-based mass consumption. Smaller households and rising 65+ share imply demand concentration in urban centers and niche luxury segments, while mass-market penetration faces structural headwinds. Retail strategies must prioritize experiential, high-margin formats and digital-first engagement to capture shrinking but higher-value consumer segments. Long-term brand penetration will require agility in assortment architecture, favoring exclusivity over volume.
