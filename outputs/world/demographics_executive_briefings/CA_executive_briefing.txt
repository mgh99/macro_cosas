**Structural Consumer Base & Demographic Momentum**
The market exhibits a structurally expanding consumer base, with total population growing at a steady 1.1% CAGR over the past decade and projected to continue rising through 2036. However, this expansion is heavily migration-dependent, as natural population change has declined sharply (from +112.6k in 2016 to +16.9k in 2022) while net migration remains volatile but critical (peaking at +459.9k in 2022). The working-age population (15–64) remains robust, but the core consumption cohort (25–49) is plateauing, signaling potential demand saturation in key apparel and luxury segments. Accelerated ageing is evident, with the 65+ cohort growing faster than younger groups, particularly the 80+ segment (+10.5% CAGR). While the 10-year projection suggests continued growth, the underlying fundamentals reveal a migration-supported equilibrium with structural risks: shrinking natural growth and ageing demographics could erode long-term demand resilience. Retail expansion strategies must prioritize migration corridors and ageing-adaptive offerings to sustain growth.

**Household Structure & Consumption Architecture**
Average household size has declined to 2.45, reflecting smaller, more individualized consumption units. This trend, combined with ageing demographics, suggests a structural shift toward premium, discretionary spending rather than broad-based mass consumption. Smaller households imply higher per-capita spending intensity but also concentration in urban centers, necessitating a shift toward experiential and convenience-driven retail formats. The ageing population further pressures demand for luxury goods, as older cohorts prioritize practicality over aspirational purchases. The market structurally favors individualized consumption expansion, but with a narrowing base due to ageing and household fragmentation. Retailers should optimize assortment architecture for smaller, high-margin transactions and invest in digital-first, localized store formats to capture this evolving demand landscape.
