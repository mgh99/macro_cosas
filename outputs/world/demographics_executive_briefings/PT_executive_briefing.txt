**Structural Consumer Base & Demographic Momentum**
The market exhibits a structurally declining demographic fundamentals, with total population projected to shrink by 1.5% over the next decade, driven by persistent negative natural change and diminishing migration offsets. The core consumption cohort (25–49) remains stable but shows no growth, while the working-age population (15–64) is gradually contracting, signaling weakening labor availability and demand resilience. Accelerated ageing is evident, with the 65+ cohort expanding at a 1.2% CAGR, and the 80+ segment growing even faster, intensifying long-term dependency pressures. Migration has been critical to mitigating decline, but its volatility and projected tapering (from +68.7k in 2022 to +16.5k by 2026) suggest unsustainable equilibrium. These trends imply constrained retail expansion, heightened competition for labor, and reduced demand elasticity, necessitating strategic shifts toward ageing and migration-dependent segments.

**Household Structure & Consumption Architecture**
Household size has declined from 2.66 in 2011 to 2.58 in 2022, reflecting smaller, more individualized consumption units. This structural shift, combined with ageing demographics, suggests a transition toward premium, discretionary spending rather than broad-based mass consumption. The shrinking core cohort and rising elderly share imply a rebalancing toward smaller, older households, likely concentrated in urban centers. While this supports niche luxury and individualized retail formats, it narrows the structural consumption base, requiring targeted assortment strategies and store formats optimized for smaller, higher-value transactions. Long-term brand penetration will depend on adapting to these demographic constraints rather than relying on population-driven growth.
