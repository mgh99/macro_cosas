**1. Structural Consumer Base & Demographic Momentum**
The market exhibits a **structurally declining demographic fundamentals** trajectory, with total population growth decelerating from 0.3% CAGR (2016–2023) to near-stagnation by 2026. Natural population change has collapsed from 156k in 2016 to a projected 3k by 2026, indicating severe fertility-driven contraction. Migration remains the sole growth driver (90k+ annually), masking underlying demographic fragility. The core consumption cohort (25–49) peaked in 2022 and is projected to shrink by 5% by 2030, while the 65+ cohort expands by 12%. Accelerated ageing (80+ cohort growing at 3.1% CAGR) signals long-term demand compression. The forward projection reveals a **migration-supported equilibrium** but with unsustainable structural risks: labour shortages and shrinking prime-age demand. Retail expansion faces structural headwinds, requiring migration-dependent strategies and labour efficiency investments.

**2. Household Structure & Consumption Architecture**
Household size has contracted from 2.25 (2011) to 2.22 (2022), reflecting smaller, ageing households. This trend, combined with the 25–49 cohort’s decline, suggests a shift toward **individualized premium consumption** over mass-market demand. Older, single-person households (65+) will dominate growth, implying demand rebalancing toward convenience, health, and experiential luxury. The narrowing consumption base—driven by ageing and shrinking households—structurally constrains broad-based growth. Retail strategies must prioritize urban, high-income micro-locations and digital-first premium assortments to offset demographic headwinds. Long-term brand penetration hinges on adapting to smaller, discretionary-spending households.
