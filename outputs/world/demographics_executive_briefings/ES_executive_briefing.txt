**Structural Consumer Base & Demographic Momentum**
The market exhibits a **structurally declining demographic fundamentals** trajectory, with total population peaking in 2024 (47.9M) and contracting to 47.1M by 2036. Natural population decline (consistently negative since 2016) and decelerating net migration (peaking in 2019 at 464.8K, then tapering to 85.3K by 2026) underscore a **migration-dependent equilibrium** that cannot offset ageing dynamics. The core consumption cohort (25–49) shrinks from 22.8% (2021) to 21.5% (2036), while the 65+ share grows from 15.2% to 18.3%. Accelerated ageing (80+ cohort expanding by 12.3% CAGR) signals rising dependency ratios, eroding labor availability and demand resilience. Long-term retail expansion faces structural headwinds, requiring strategic shifts toward ageing-centric and migration-adaptive markets.

**Household Structure & Consumption Architecture**
Household size trends (declining from 2.69 in 2011 to 2.58 in 2022) indicate a shift toward **individualized consumption**, with smaller, older households dominating. This rebalancing favors premium, discretionary spending over mass-market demand, as ageing cohorts (65+) grow faster than working-age groups. The structural narrowing of the consumption base suggests a transition from family-driven demand to **ageing and smaller-household rebalancing**, necessitating store format adaptations (e.g., urban micro-locations, experiential retail) and assortment shifts toward longevity-focused luxury. Brand penetration strategies must prioritize high-margin, individualized offerings to offset declining mass-market potential.
