**Executive Market Diagnostic: Structural Assessment of Purchasing Power, Tourism Monetization, and Demographic Sustainability**

The market exhibits a structurally constrained demographic profile, with persistent negative natural population change offset only partially by declining net migration, leading to a projected 6.5% population decline by 2036. Core working-age cohorts (25–54) are shrinking, while aging cohorts (60+) expand, signaling long-term consumer base erosion. Household sizes remain stable (2.4 persons), but declining migration and low birth rates undermine future demand sustainability. Economically, tourism remains a critical growth driver, with inbound expenditure per arrival rising sharply post-pandemic (e.g., +120% from 2020 to 2024), though dependency on external demand is high (tourism’s share of GDP not provided but implied by expenditure trends). Domestic overnight stays lag behind inbound volumes, suggesting limited domestic monetization. Inflation and unemployment data are absent, but consumer confidence trends would be critical to assess short-term volatility. Strategically, the market is **premium-concentrated but demographically constrained**, with tourism as a cyclical growth lever but vulnerable to external shocks and long-term consumer erosion. Prioritization of high-value tourism segments and domestic demand stimulation is essential to offset structural headwinds.
