**Executive Market Diagnostic: Structural Assessment of Purchasing Power, Tourism Monetization, and Demographic Sustainability**

The market exhibits a structurally constrained demographic profile, with persistent negative natural population change offset only by moderate net migration, leading to a projected long-term decline in total population. Core working-age cohorts (25–54) remain stable but show gradual erosion, while aging trends (65+) accelerate, raising concerns over long-term consumer sustainability. Household sizes are shrinking, further pressuring per capita purchasing power. Economic fundamentals reveal moderate GDP growth but with inflationary pressures, as evidenced by rising consumer prices, while labor market stability (unemployment) remains a critical variable. Tourism monetization is robust, with high expenditure per arrival and strong recovery post-pandemic, though dependency on inbound visitors (tourism’s share of GDP) introduces external exposure risks. The market’s strategic positioning is **premium-concentrated but demographically constrained**, with tourism-driven growth potential tempered by structural population decline and household fragmentation. Short-term cyclicality in consumer confidence and tourism demand must be managed against a backdrop of long-term demographic headwinds.
