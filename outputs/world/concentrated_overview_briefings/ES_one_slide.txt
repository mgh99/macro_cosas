**Executive Market Diagnostic: Structural Assessment of Purchasing Power, Tourism Monetization, and Demographic Sustainability**

The market exhibits a structurally constrained economic environment, with stagnant GDP growth and persistent negative natural population change, partially offset by net migration inflows. While GDP per capita remains moderate, inflationary pressures and elevated youth unemployment suggest limited disposable income expansion, particularly among core working-age cohorts (25–54). Tourism monetization is highly concentrated, with inbound arrivals and expenditure rebounding post-pandemic but heavily reliant on European and American visitors, indicating external dependency. The average length of stay and high accommodation overnights signal strong monetization potential, though cyclical volatility remains a risk. Demographically, the population is aging, with shrinking working-age cohorts and declining household sizes, undermining long-term consumer sustainability. Strategic positioning: a **premium-concentrated but cyclical market**, externally dependent on tourism and demographically constrained, requiring targeted high-value segmentation to offset structural headwinds.
