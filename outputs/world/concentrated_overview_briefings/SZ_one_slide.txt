**Executive Market Diagnostic: Structural Assessment of Purchasing Power, Tourism Monetization, and Demographic Sustainability**

The market exhibits moderate purchasing power dynamics, with GDP per capita growth outpacing inflation but constrained by persistent youth unemployment, which may limit discretionary spending. Tourism monetization is highly concentrated, with inbound arrivals from Africa and land-based transport dominating, yet expenditure per arrival remains volatile, reflecting external shocks. The demographic profile is structurally favorable, with a growing working-age population (20–54) and stable household sizes, though net migration outflows and aging cohorts beyond 2030 could pressure long-term demand. Strategically, the market is **premium-concentrated but cyclically sensitive**, leveraging tourism dependency and a youthful consumer base while requiring hedges against external demand volatility and demographic shifts. Structural attractiveness is contingent on sustaining high-value tourism flows and mitigating labor market inefficiencies.
