**Executive Market Diagnostic: Structural Assessment of Purchasing Power, Tourism Monetization, and Demographic Sustainability**

The market exhibits moderate purchasing power dynamics, with GDP growth stabilizing post-pandemic but constrained by inflationary pressures and labor market volatility. While GDP per capita suggests a mid-tier income profile, consumer confidence remains fragile, reflecting income inequality and household financial stress. Tourism monetization is highly concentrated, with inbound arrivals and expenditure recovering strongly, yet dependency on European and American visitors introduces cyclical risk. The high value per arrival (e.g., €850+ in 2024) underscores premium tourism potential, though short-stay structures (2.5–3 nights) limit ancillary spending. Demographically, the population is aging, with declining natural growth offset by net migration (90k+ annually), but core 25–54 cohorts are shrinking, weakening long-term consumer sustainability. Household sizes (2.2–2.3 persons) suggest limited multi-generational spending power. Structurally, the market is **premium-concentrated but cyclically exposed**, with tourism as a dominant growth lever but demographic headwinds constraining domestic demand. Strategic positioning should prioritize high-value tourism segments while mitigating external shocks and addressing demographic erosion through targeted migration policies.
