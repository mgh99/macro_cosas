**Executive Market Diagnostic: Structural Assessment of Purchasing Power, Tourism Monetization, and Demographic Sustainability**

The market exhibits moderate purchasing power dynamics, with GDP per capita growth outpacing inflation (HICP) but constrained by labor market volatility (unemployment rate fluctuations). Tourism monetization is robust, with high inbound expenditure per arrival (e.g., $2,780 in 2024) and a growing share of tourism GDP (UN_Tourism_8_9_1_TDGDP_04_2025), though dependency on external demand (inbound arrivals) introduces cyclicality. Demographic momentum is stable, with net migration (433.8k in 2023) offsetting natural population decline, sustaining a working-age cohort (25–54) that remains the largest demographic segment. Household size (2.45) and aging trends (65+ growth) suggest long-term consumer sustainability but require premium segmentation to align with evolving preferences. Strategically, the market is **premium-concentrated and externally dependent**, with scalable tourism-driven demand but vulnerable to global shocks. Structural attractiveness hinges on monetizing high-value inbound tourism while mitigating demographic and labor market risks.
