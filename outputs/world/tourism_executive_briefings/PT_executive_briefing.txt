**1. Structural Attractiveness & Exposure Profile**
The tourism sector’s direct GDP contribution has exhibited moderate but steady growth (CAGR ~2.5%), signaling embedded structural relevance rather than cyclical volatility. Inbound arrivals have recovered post-2020, with a 10-year CAGR of 4.2%, driven primarily by Europe (75% of arrivals) and the Americas (15%), exposing the market to geopolitical and regional demand shocks. Domestic trips (CAGR 3.1%) provide partial buffering but remain secondary to inbound flows, classifying the market as a **regionally dependent growth hub** with high external exposure. This profile suggests elevated volatility sensitivity, necessitating cautious long-term retail expansion, particularly in premium segments reliant on long-haul European travelers.

**2. Demand Architecture & Operational Reliability**
Air transport dominates (90% of arrivals), indicating premium accessibility and long-haul penetration, though this exposes retail to airfare sensitivity. Average length of stay (5.2 nights) and hotel overnights (15.5M) support retail interaction time, but domestic accommodation (10.5M overnights) acts as a stabilizer. The architecture leans toward **air-exposed luxury inflow potential**, with predictable retail throughput but staffing and network planning contingent on air travel resilience. Short-stay transactional risks are mitigated by multi-night stays, but reliance on European demand introduces operational fragility.

**3. Value Intensity & Luxury Monetization Potential**
Expenditure per arrival ($2,800) has outpaced arrival growth (CAGR 5.1% vs. 4.2%), signaling value-accretive tourism. Business travel (30% of arrivals) and high discretionary spending align with **high-margin luxury capture**, though personal travel (70%) suggests aspirational consumption. The long-stay profile (5.2 nights) enhances monetization, but regional concentration risks dilute premium positioning. Strategically, tourism underpins luxury retail’s long-term relevance, though diversification into emerging regions is critical to sustain revenue quality.
