**1. Tourism Volume & Composition**
The tourism demand base is large and internationally diversified, with total arrivals recovering strongly post-pandemic, reaching 116.2 million in 2024—a 10% increase from 2023. Foreign arrivals (322.2 million nights in 2024) now exceed domestic (182.9 million), reflecting a structurally internationalized market. Key origin markets include the EU27 (31.8 million arrivals in 2024), the UK (11.3 million), and the US (4.5 million), with broad-based growth across Europe and North America. While China and Asia show volatility, the diversification mitigates concentration risks. This suggests exposure to global demand cycles, with resilience to regional shocks. The demand base is thus *large and internationally diversified*, supporting retail’s geographic reach and revenue stability.

**2. Seasonality & Stability**
The seasonality ratio has declined from 54.8% in 2020 to 36.0% in 2024, classifying the market as *moderately seasonal*. Peak periods (top 3 months) now account for less than 40% of annual nights, reducing volatility. However, retail demand remains cyclically concentrated, with inventory and staffing needing alignment to seasonal peaks. The shift toward a more balanced distribution suggests improved stability, but peak months still drive disproportionate revenue. Operational planning must balance efficiency with peak-period capacity, ensuring store performance aligns with demand fluctuations.

**3. Economic Impact & Spending Quality**
Average expenditure per trip has not been provided, but the data reveals a high-value tourism structure. Business travel (8.6 million trips in 2024) complements leisure, while extended stays (4+ nights: 467.8 million nights in 2024) dominate, indicating premium potential. The mix of personal and professional travel, coupled with longer durations, supports *high-value discretionary spending*. This aligns with luxury retail’s focus on affluent, high-spending tourists, though reliance on extended stays may limit high-frequency volume. The tourism structure thus enhances strategic relevance for apparel and luxury sectors.
