**1. Tourism Volume & Composition**
The tourism demand base is large and internationally diversified, with total arrivals growing steadily over the past five years, particularly from key markets such as the U.S., China, and the EU. Foreign arrivals have rebounded strongly post-pandemic, accounting for a significant share of total nights spent, indicating high international exposure. The diversification of origin markets—with notable contributions from North America, Asia, and Europe—reduces reliance on any single region, though concentration risks persist in markets like China and the U.S. The growth trajectory suggests broad-based demand rather than dependency on specific countries, positioning the destination as a resilient hub for global tourism. This international diversification mitigates exposure to regional economic shocks but requires strategic alignment with fluctuating global demand cycles.

**2. Seasonality & Stability**
Tourism demand exhibits moderate seasonality, with the seasonality ratio stabilizing below 35% in recent years, indicating a shift toward more balanced annual distribution. While peak periods still drive disproportionate revenue, the reduced concentration in the top three months suggests operational stability for retail. This trend lowers volatility risks for inventory planning and staffing, though peak seasons remain critical for maximizing revenue. The transition from highly seasonal to moderately seasonal demand implies a more predictable retail environment, though luxury and apparel retailers should still optimize for seasonal spikes to capitalize on high-spend periods.

**3. Economic Impact & Spending Quality**
The tourism structure supports high-value discretionary spending, with average expenditure per trip showing steady growth over five years. The mix of personal and business travel leans heavily toward leisure, while the prevalence of longer stays (4+ nights) suggests higher spending potential. This aligns with premium retail demand, as extended visits correlate with increased exposure to luxury and apparel offerings. The combination of rising expenditure and a shift toward longer stays underscores the strategic relevance of tourism for high-margin retail sectors, positioning the destination as a lucrative market for apparel and luxury brands.
