**1. Tourism Volume & Composition**
The tourism demand base is large and internationally diversified, with total arrivals recovering strongly post-2020, reaching 146.2 million in 2024—a 22% increase from 2022. Domestic travelers dominate (78% of total arrivals in 2024), but foreign arrivals have rebounded sharply, particularly from Europe (62% of foreign arrivals) and North America (12%). Key markets like the U.S., Switzerland, and the UK show robust growth, while China and Russia exhibit volatility. The share of intra-EU travelers remains stable, mitigating concentration risks. This structure suggests exposure to global demand cycles, with domestic demand providing stability but international visitors driving premium spending. The demand base is thus *large and internationally diversified*, though domestic reliance persists.

**2. Seasonality & Stability**
The seasonality ratio has declined from 42% in 2020 to 30% in 2024, classifying the market as *low-seasonal* (≤30%). This indicates stable demand distribution, reducing volatility risks for retail operations. Peak periods no longer disproportionately drive revenue, allowing for balanced inventory planning and staffing. The shift suggests structural stability, with retail demand less prone to cyclical concentration. However, operational efficiency gains may require adjustments to align with the now-flatter demand curve.

**3. Economic Impact & Spending Quality**
Average expenditure per trip has not been provided, but the data reveals a high share of extended stays (4+ nights: 65% of total nights in 2024) and a 65% personal vs. 35% business travel split. This structure supports *high-value discretionary spending*, as longer stays and leisure travel correlate with premium retail engagement. The absence of expenditure figures limits precise conclusions, but the mix of domestic stability and international premium demand positions tourism as strategically relevant for apparel and luxury retail. The absence of low-margin volume tourism further enhances the sector’s appeal.
