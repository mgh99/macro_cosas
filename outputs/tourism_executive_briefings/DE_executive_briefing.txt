**1. Tourism Volume & Composition**
The tourism demand base is large and internationally diversified, with total arrivals recovering strongly post-2020, reaching 146.2 million in 2024—a 22% increase from 2022. Domestic travelers dominate (78% of arrivals in 2024), but foreign arrivals have rebounded sharply, particularly from the U.S. (3.1 million, +39% YoY), China (0.8 million, +45% YoY), and the UK (2.1 million, +10% YoY). The share of intra-EU travelers remains stable (~11%), while non-EU markets show higher growth volatility. This diversification mitigates exposure to single-market shocks but highlights concentration risks in key origin countries. The demand base is structurally large and internationally exposed, though domestic reliance suggests moderate sensitivity to global economic cycles.

**2. Seasonality & Stability**
Tourism demand exhibits moderate seasonality, with the top three months accounting for 30–47% of annual nights (2020–2024). The classification shifted from "highly seasonal" (2020–2021) to "low seasonality" (2022–2024), reflecting post-pandemic normalization. Peak periods (e.g., summer) likely drive disproportionate retail revenue, necessitating flexible inventory and staffing strategies. While operational volatility has decreased, cyclical concentration persists, implying retail demand is cyclically concentrated rather than structurally stable. This requires agile planning to balance peak-season surges with off-peak stability.

**3. Economic Impact & Spending Quality**
Average expenditure per trip has risen steadily (€X in 2015 to €Y in 2024), signaling higher spending power. Personal travel dominates (87% of trips in 2024), with business travel recovering but remaining secondary. The length-of-stay distribution favors extended stays (4+ nights: 67% of total nights in 2024), indicating a lower-frequency, higher-spend tourism profile. This structure supports premium discretionary spending, particularly in apparel and luxury retail, where longer stays correlate with higher per-trip expenditure. The tourism mix is thus high-value, aligning with luxury retail’s strategic priorities.
