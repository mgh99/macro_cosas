**1. Tourism Volume & Composition**
The tourism demand base is large and internationally diversified, with total arrivals growing from 59.7 million in 2020 to 120.2 million in 2024, reflecting a robust recovery post-2020. Domestic travelers account for approximately 68% of total nights spent, while foreign visitors contribute 32%, indicating a balanced but domestically anchored market. The origin markets are broadly diversified, with significant contributions from Europe (EU27: 15.4% of arrivals in 2024), North America (4.1%), and Asia (2.6%). Growth is broad-based, with no single country dominating, though intra-EU travel remains a key driver. This structure suggests moderate exposure to global demand cycles, with resilience derived from diversified sources but potential vulnerability to regional economic downturns.

**2. Seasonality & Stability**
The seasonality ratio has declined from 45.5% in 2020 (highly seasonal) to 31.0% in 2024 (low seasonality), indicating a shift toward a more stable demand pattern. However, peak periods still drive disproportionate revenue, as evidenced by the concentration of nights in the top three months. This implies operational volatility for retail, requiring flexible inventory and staffing strategies to manage peak demand surges. While the trend toward lower seasonality is favorable, retailers must remain prepared for cyclical concentration, particularly in high-traffic urban and coastal hubs.

**3. Economic Impact & Spending Quality**
Average expenditure per trip has not been provided, but the data suggests a mix of personal (87% of trips) and business (13%) travel, with a notable increase in business trips post-2020. The length-of-stay distribution reveals a higher proportion of short stays (1–3 nights: 56% of total nights in 2024), which may indicate high-frequency, lower-spend tourism. However, the presence of extended stays (4+ nights: 44%) suggests potential for premium discretionary spending, particularly among international visitors. The tourism structure supports a blend of stable mid-market consumption and high-value opportunities, though retailers must tailor strategies to capture both segments effectively.
