**Structural Consumer Base & Demographic Momentum**

The market exhibits a **structurally declining demographic fundamentals** trajectory, with total population growth decelerating from 0.3% CAGR (2016–2023) to near stagnation by 2030. Natural population change has collapsed (2026 projection: +2.9k), while net migration (2023–2027: ~90k/year) becomes the sole driver of growth. The core consumption cohort (25–49) is shrinking (2021–2023: -3.3% for 45–49), while the working-age share (15–64) declines from 63% (2021) to 61% (2026). Accelerated ageing is evident in the 65+ cohort’s 10-year growth (2021–2031: +1.2M), with the 80+ segment expanding at 2.1% CAGR. The 10-year projection confirms a **migration-supported equilibrium**, but structural demand resilience is at risk due to shrinking prime-age cohorts and rising dependency ratios. Retail expansion faces labor shortages and demand compression, necessitating migration-dependent strategies or high-value niche targeting.

**Household Structure & Consumption Architecture**

The market is transitioning toward **individualized consumption expansion**, with average household size shrinking from 2.25 (2011) to 2.22 (2015) and likely continuing downward. Smaller households (1–2 persons) now dominate, aligning with ageing dynamics (65+ growth) and urbanization. This shift favors premium, discretionary spending over mass-market demand, as older, single-person households prioritize quality over quantity. However, structurally constrained growth looms: declining core cohorts and ageing reduce broad-based demand, while migration-dependent growth may not offset shrinking domestic consumption. Retail strategies must pivot to **smaller, high-margin formats** and experiential luxury, abandoning mass-market expansion assumptions. Long-term brand penetration hinges on capturing ageing, affluent segments and migration-driven urban clusters.
