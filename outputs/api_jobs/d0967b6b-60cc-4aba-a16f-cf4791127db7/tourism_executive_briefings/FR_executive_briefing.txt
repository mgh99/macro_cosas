**1. Structural Attractiveness & Exposure Profile**
The tourism sector contributes 3.5–4.0% of GDP, reflecting moderate macroeconomic weight with limited systemic exposure. Inbound arrivals show secular growth (CAGR ~3.5% pre-pandemic) but remain vulnerable to shocks, as evidenced by the 2020 collapse and uneven recovery. Geographic concentration is pronounced, with Europe accounting for ~80% of arrivals, creating regional dependency risks. Domestic demand (277M trips in 2024) acts as a partial buffer, but its volatility (e.g., 2020 drop to 212M) underscores external exposure. The profile aligns with a *regionally dependent growth market*, where luxury retail expansion must account for cyclical volatility and concentration risks, particularly in high-value European corridors.

**2. Demand Architecture & Operational Reliability**
The transport mix (70% land, 30% air) suggests limited premium accessibility, with short-haul dominance reducing long-haul luxury potential. Average length of stay (derived from overnights/arrivals) is ~5 nights, supporting moderate retail interaction time, though hotel overnights (77M in 2024) indicate stable accommodation demand. Domestic overnights (137M in 2024) provide some demand stabilization but are insufficient to offset inbound shocks. The architecture offers *short-stay transactional tourism* with predictable throughput but limited premium penetration, necessitating lean staffing and network flexibility to manage volatility.

**3. Value Intensity & Luxury Monetization Potential**
Expenditure per arrival ($1,000–$1,200) grew faster than arrivals (CAGR ~4.2% vs. ~3.5%), signaling monetization intensity. The business-to-personal mix (~12% business) is low, limiting premium positioning. Length of stay suggests discretionary spending potential, but the transactional nature of demand aligns with *aspirational mid-market retail* rather than high-margin luxury capture. Tourism’s strategic relevance is conditional: it supports stable mid-tier consumption but requires targeted luxury strategies to elevate value capture, particularly in European corridors where spending is concentrated.
