**1. Economic Scale, Growth Trajectory & Forward Momentum**
The economy exhibits a mature but stable consumption base, with GDP growth stabilizing near 2.5% annually over the past five years and projected to maintain this trajectory over the next five years. GDP per capita has grown at a 3.1% CAGR, signaling steady purchasing power expansion, though deceleration in recent years suggests diminishing structural momentum. The absence of acceleration in either GDP or per capita metrics indicates a plateauing growth environment, limiting high-potential expansion opportunities. Retail expansion should prioritize efficiency over aggressive scaling, with a focus on margin resilience in a stable but unremarkable growth context.

**2. Stability, Inflation & Cyclical Risk Profile**
Labor market conditions are improving, with youth unemployment declining at an 8.4% CAGR, reducing generational demand pressure. However, the 24.7% youth unemployment rate remains elevated, posing a medium-term risk to discretionary spending. Inflation has moderated, with the Harmonised Index of Consumer Prices stabilizing near 2.1% annually, mitigating real income erosion. Consumer confidence is flat (99.8 latest), signaling cautious optimism but limited upside for luxury demand. The environment is consumption-supportive but lacks cyclical dynamism, necessitating pricing strategies that balance affordability and margin preservation.

**3. Income Structure & Luxury Market Depth**
Income distribution trends reveal a narrowing spread (22.3 pp) between the top and bottom deciles, with the top 10% share declining at a 0.6% CAGR and the bottom 10% share rising at 0.9% CAGR. This suggests a gradual shift toward broader middle-class participation, though GDP per capita ($42,300 latest) remains insufficient to support deep premium capture. The market structurally favors a broad mid-market retail opportunity, with polarized luxury demand limited to niche segments. Assortment positioning should prioritize accessible premium offerings, while long-term brand scaling must balance aspirational appeal with mass-market accessibility.
