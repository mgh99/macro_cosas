**1. Economic Scale, Growth Trajectory & Forward Momentum**
The economy exhibits a mature but stable consumption base, with GDP growth stabilizing around 2.1% (5-year CAGR) and projected to remain flat over the next five years. GDP per capita, while elevated at $52,300 (2025), shows decelerating growth (1.8% CAGR), signaling plateauing purchasing power depth. The absence of structural acceleration suggests limited upside for broad-based retail expansion, though the economy’s size and stability mitigate downside risk. The lack of cyclical rebounds in GDP growth indicates a structurally constrained macro landscape, where medium-term retail confidence should prioritize defensive positioning over aggressive scaling.

**2. Stability, Inflation & Cyclical Risk Profile**
Labor market conditions are mixed, with youth unemployment declining (18.9% in 2025, -1.5% CAGR) but remaining elevated, signaling generational demand pressure. Consumer confidence (98.3) is flat, reflecting muted discretionary spending sentiment. Inflation (2.3% HICP in 2025) is stabilizing, but real income erosion persists due to stagnant wage growth. The environment is cyclically exposed, with limited pricing power and margin resilience. Retail strategies should emphasize cost discipline and inventory agility to navigate short-term revenue visibility challenges.

**3. Income Structure & Luxury Market Depth**
Income distribution is highly concentrated, with the top 10% holding 25.0% of income (22.0 pp spread over the bottom 10%), indicating a polarized luxury niche. While GDP per capita supports premium capture, the shrinking income share of the top 10% (-1.3% CAGR) suggests eroding high-end demand. The absence of middle-class depth (bottom 10% share at 3.0%) limits broad aspirational consumption. Assortment positioning should prioritize ultra-premium segments, with price architecture emphasizing exclusivity over volume. Long-term brand scaling must balance niche appeal with controlled expansion to avoid structural demand saturation.
