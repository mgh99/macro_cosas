**Executive Market Diagnostic: Structural Assessment of Purchasing Power, Tourism Monetization, and Demographic Sustainability**

The market exhibits a structurally constrained economic environment, with declining population trends (negative natural change and moderating net migration) and an aging demographic profile, as evidenced by shrinking working-age cohorts (20–54) and growing elderly segments (65+). Household sizes remain stable but offer limited growth leverage, while GDP per capita and consumer confidence suggest moderate purchasing power with inflationary pressures. Tourism monetization is highly concentrated in inbound arrivals, particularly from Europe and the Americas, with strong expenditure per arrival and extended stays, but dependency on external demand introduces cyclical volatility. Long-term consumer sustainability is challenged by demographic contraction and limited domestic demand, as domestic accommodation overnights lag behind inbound metrics. Strategically, the market is **premium-concentrated but cyclically exposed**, with tourism as a critical growth driver offset by structural demographic headwinds. Leadership should prioritize high-value tourism segments while mitigating reliance on external demand through domestic market diversification.
