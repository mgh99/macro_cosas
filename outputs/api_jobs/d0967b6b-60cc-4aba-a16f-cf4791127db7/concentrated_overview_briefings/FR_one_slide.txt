**Executive Market Diagnostic: Structural Assessment of Purchasing Power, Tourism Monetization, and Demographic Sustainability**

The market exhibits moderate economic resilience with steady GDP growth and stable inflation, though labor market dynamics—reflected in youth unemployment—suggest potential constraints on disposable income expansion. Tourism remains a critical growth lever, with high inbound expenditure per arrival and a strong dependency on international visitors, particularly from Europe. However, volatility in accommodation overnights and regional arrival patterns indicates exposure to external shocks. Demographically, the population is aging gradually, with net migration offsetting natural decline, but forward projections show slowing growth. Household structures are compact, supporting higher per-capita spending power. Structurally, the market is **premium-concentrated and externally dependent**, with long-term consumer sustainability hinging on sustained tourism demand and labor market stability. Strategic positioning should prioritize high-value tourism monetization while mitigating cyclical risks and demographic headwinds.
