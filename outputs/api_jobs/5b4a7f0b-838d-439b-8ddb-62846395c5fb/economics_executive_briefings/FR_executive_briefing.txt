**1. Economic Scale, Growth Trajectory & Forward Momentum**
The economy exhibits a mature but stable consumption base, with GDP growth stabilizing around 2.0% annually over the past five years and projected to maintain this trajectory over the next five years. GDP per capita, a critical proxy for purchasing power depth, has grown at a 1.8% CAGR, reflecting steady but unaccelerated expansion. While inflation-adjusted growth remains modest, the absence of deceleration suggests structural resilience rather than cyclical fragility. The economy is not a high-potential expansion market but avoids structural constraints, positioning it as a stable mid-term retail opportunity. Retail expansion should prioritize sustainable, margin-conscious strategies over aggressive scaling, leveraging steady demand rather than speculative growth.

**2. Stability, Inflation & Cyclical Risk Profile**
Labor market conditions are stable, with unemployment trending downward (5-year CAGR: -1.5%) and youth unemployment declining, though elevated at 18.9% in 2025. This suggests generational demand pressure remains a medium-term risk. Inflation, as measured by the Harmonised Index of Consumer Prices, is projected to stabilize near 2.5% annually, mitigating real income erosion. Consumer confidence (98.27 in 2025) is flat but resilient, indicating stable discretionary spending sentiment. The environment supports stable real consumption growth, though cyclical risks are present. Retail pricing power and margin resilience will depend on disciplined inventory management and promotional discipline to offset inflationary pressures.

**3. Income Structure & Luxury Market Depth**
Income distribution reveals a polarized structure, with the top 10% holding 25.0% of income (CAGR: -1.3%) and the bottom 10% holding just 3.0% (CAGR: -1.28%). The 22.0 percentage point spread between these cohorts suggests a luxury market concentrated among high earners, with limited broad-based aspirational demand. GDP per capita ($45,000 in 2025) aligns with mid-tier luxury penetration, but the income architecture favors premium capture over mass-market expansion. Retail strategies should prioritize high-margin, exclusive assortments and tiered pricing to maximize revenue from the top decile, while avoiding overinvestment in mid-market segments. Long-term brand scaling will hinge on deepening relationships with affluent consumers rather than chasing volume.
