**Executive Market Diagnostic: Structural Assessment of Purchasing Power, Tourism Monetization, and Demographic Sustainability**

The market exhibits moderate purchasing power dynamics, with GDP per capita growth outpacing inflation but constrained by labor market volatility, as evidenced by fluctuating unemployment rates. Tourism monetization is robust, with high expenditure per arrival and a strong dependency on inbound visitors, particularly from Europe, though external shocks (e.g., 2020’s sharp decline in arrivals) highlight vulnerability. Demographic momentum is stable but decelerating, with net migration offsetting natural population decline, though aging cohorts (e.g., 65+ growth) may pressure long-term consumer sustainability. Household structures remain compact, supporting disposable income efficiency.

Strategically, this market is **premium-concentrated but cyclically sensitive**, with strong tourism-driven revenue potential but structural exposure to external demand shocks and demographic aging. Short-term volatility notwithstanding, its monetization intensity and working-age population base suggest scalability for luxury apparel, provided supply chains adapt to shifting inbound travel patterns and domestic consumption trends.
