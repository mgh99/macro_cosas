**1. Economic Scale, Growth Trajectory & Forward Momentum**
The economy exhibits a mature but stable consumption base, with GDP growth stabilizing around 2.1% (5-year CAGR) and projected to maintain this trajectory over the next five years. GDP per capita growth (1.8% CAGR) suggests moderate purchasing power expansion, though structural constraints limit acceleration. The absence of cyclical rebounds indicates a steady-state environment rather than post-shock normalization. While not a high-potential market, the economy’s resilience supports medium-term retail expansion confidence, particularly in defensive categories. Luxury segments may face slower growth without significant per capita acceleration.

**2. Stability, Inflation & Cyclical Risk Profile**
Unemployment trends (5-year CAGR: -1.52%) and youth unemployment (18.89% in 2025) signal labor market fragility, particularly for aspirational demographics. Inflation projections (HICP) remain subdued, mitigating real income erosion but also limiting pricing power. Consumer confidence (98.269 in 2025) is flat, reflecting cautious discretionary spending sentiment. The environment is cyclically exposed, requiring flexible pricing strategies and inventory discipline to preserve margins. Short-term revenue visibility is constrained by generational demand pressures.

**3. Income Structure & Luxury Market Depth**
The income distribution spread (22.0 pp) and declining top 10% share (5-year CAGR: -1.31%) indicate a polarized structure, favoring high-end luxury concentration over broad middle-class depth. GDP per capita ($42,500 in 2025) aligns with premium capture potential, but the shrinking income share of the lowest 10% (3.0%) suggests limited aspirational demand. The market structurally favors a polarized luxury niche, necessitating targeted assortment positioning and tiered price architecture. Long-term brand scaling should prioritize exclusivity over mass-market penetration.
