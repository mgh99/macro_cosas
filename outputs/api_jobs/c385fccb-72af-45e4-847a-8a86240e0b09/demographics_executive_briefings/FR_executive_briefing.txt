**Structural Consumer Base & Demographic Momentum**
The market exhibits a **structurally declining demographic fundamentals** trajectory, with total population growth decelerating from 0.3% CAGR (2016–2023) to near stagnation (0.1% CAGR projected 2024–2036). Natural population change has collapsed to near-zero by 2026, signaling an unsustainable fertility-driven expansion. Migration remains the sole growth driver (90k–179k annually), masking underlying contraction. The working-age population (15–64) is shrinking as a share of total population, while the 65+ cohort grows at 1.2% CAGR, accelerating ageing dynamics. The core consumption cohort (25–49) is stagnant, with 2023 data showing a 1.3% decline in 30–34-year-olds and 3.9% drop in 45–49-year-olds. Forward projections confirm this trend, with 2036 population growth entirely migration-dependent. Implications: **retail expansion faces structural headwinds**, labor shortages will intensify, and demand resilience hinges on migration inflows. Strategic focus must shift to efficiency and high-value segments to offset shrinking mass-market potential.

**Household Structure & Consumption Architecture**
Household size has contracted from 2.25 (2011) to 2.22 (2015), signaling a shift toward smaller, individualized consumption units. Combined with ageing, this implies a structural rebalancing toward **premium, discretionary spending** over family-driven mass consumption. The 25–49 cohort’s stagnation and 65+ growth suggest demand will concentrate in urban, single-person households, favoring convenience and experiential luxury. However, the shrinking 30–34 and 45–49 cohorts—key for family-oriented categories—indicate erosion of traditional demand pillars. The market structurally favors **individualized consumption expansion**, but with narrowing mass-market appeal. Retail strategies must prioritize **smaller store formats, digital integration, and premium assortments** to align with ageing, urbanized, and smaller-household trends. Long-term brand penetration will depend on adaptability to these demographic shifts.
