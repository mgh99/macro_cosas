**Executive Market Diagnostic: Structural Assessment of Purchasing Power, Tourism Monetization, and Demographic Sustainability**

The market exhibits moderate economic scale with stable GDP growth and controlled inflation, though labor market dynamics—reflected in youth unemployment—suggest potential constraints on disposable income expansion. Tourism monetization is robust, with high expenditure per arrival and strong inbound demand, particularly from Europe, though dependency on external arrivals introduces cyclical volatility. Demographically, the population is aging, with declining natural growth and reliance on net migration to sustain momentum; however, the working-age cohorts (25–54) remain substantial, supporting medium-term consumer resilience. Household structures are compact, aligning with urbanized, premium consumption patterns. Strategically, the market is **premium-concentrated with scalable tourism-driven demand but structurally exposed to external shocks and demographic transition risks**. Long-term sustainability hinges on mitigating labor market inefficiencies and diversifying growth drivers beyond tourism.
