**1. Structural Attractiveness & Exposure Profile**
The tourism sector contributes 3.5–4.0% of GDP, reflecting moderate macroeconomic weight with limited systemic exposure. Inbound arrivals exhibit secular growth (CAGR ~3.5% 2016–2019) but sharp cyclical volatility (e.g., 2020 collapse to 2021 recovery). Geographic concentration is pronounced, with Europe accounting for ~80% of inbound arrivals, creating regional dependency risks. Domestic demand (277M trips in 2024) partially buffers external shocks, but inbound flows remain structurally dominant. The profile aligns with a *regionally dependent growth market*, implying moderate volatility sensitivity and cautious long-term retail expansion confidence, particularly in high-value urban hubs.

**2. Demand Architecture & Operational Reliability**
The transport mix (55% land, 30% air) suggests strong regional accessibility but limited long-haul premium penetration. Average length of stay (derived from overnights/arrivals) is ~5 nights, supporting retail interaction time, though hotel overnights (77K in 2024) remain below pre-2020 peaks. Domestic accommodation (137K overnights in 2024) acts as a stabilizer but cannot offset inbound volatility. The architecture offers *moderate operational visibility* with transactional bias, necessitating flexible staffing and network planning to align with seasonal inbound peaks.

**3. Value Intensity & Luxury Monetization Potential**
Expenditure per arrival ($2,500–$3,000 in 2023–2024) exceeds regional benchmarks, driven by a 90% personal travel mix. However, expenditure growth (CAGR ~4.2%) outpaces arrival growth (~3.5%), signaling monetization intensity. Length of stay supports discretionary spending, but the absence of business travel (10% of trips) limits premium positioning. The model structurally supports *stable aspirational consumption* rather than high-margin luxury capture, making tourism a complementary but not primary driver for luxury retail positioning. Strategic relevance is highest in gateway cities with high inbound exposure.
