**1. Economic Scale, Growth Trajectory & Forward Momentum**
The economy exhibits a mature but stable consumption base, with GDP growth stabilizing around 2.1% (5-year CAGR) and projected to maintain this trajectory over the next five years. While GDP per capita has grown modestly (1.8% CAGR), the forward outlook suggests limited acceleration, indicating a structurally constrained macro landscape. The absence of accelerating growth trends—despite post-shock normalization—suggests retail expansion should prioritize margin resilience over aggressive footprint expansion. The lack of structural upside in purchasing power depth implies that medium-term retail confidence should focus on operational efficiency rather than aggressive market penetration.

**2. Stability, Inflation & Cyclical Risk Profile**
Labor market conditions are stable but reveal cyclical fragility, with youth unemployment declining (1.52% CAGR) but remaining elevated at 18.9%. Consumer confidence (98.27) has flattened, signaling muted discretionary spending sentiment. Inflation projections (HICP) indicate stabilization, but real income erosion risks persist. The combination of stable but uninspiring labor markets and flat confidence suggests a consumption-supportive but inflation-constrained environment. Retail pricing power will depend on cost discipline, as margin resilience is likely to face pressure from subdued demand elasticity.

**3. Income Structure & Luxury Market Depth**
Income distribution reveals a polarized structure, with the top 10% holding 25.0% of income (down 1.31% CAGR) and the bottom 10% at 3.0% (down 1.28% CAGR). The 22.0 percentage point spread between top and bottom deciles underscores a structurally limited discretionary upside, favoring high-end luxury concentration over broad-based aspirational demand. GDP per capita ($42,500, 2025) aligns with mid-market retail opportunity, but income inequality suggests assortment positioning should prioritize premium segments. Long-term brand scaling must balance exclusivity with accessible luxury to capture polarized demand.
