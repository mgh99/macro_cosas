**1. Economic Scale, Growth Trajectory & Forward Momentum**
The economy exhibits a mature but stabilizing growth profile, with GDP at current prices reflecting steady expansion over the past decade (IMF). While real GDP growth has moderated from prior peaks, the five-year CAGR aligns with long-term trends, suggesting structural stabilization rather than deceleration. GDP per capita, a critical proxy for purchasing power depth, shows gradual upward momentum, though forward projections indicate only modest acceleration. This signals a consumption base that is expanding but not accelerating, positioning the market as a mature, stable environment for retail. The absence of cyclical rebounds or structural constraints implies medium-term expansion confidence, though with lower upside potential compared to high-growth emerging markets. Retail strategies should prioritize sustainable, value-driven expansion over aggressive scaling.

**2. Stability, Inflation & Cyclical Risk Profile**
Labor market indicators reveal gradual improvement, with unemployment trending downward and youth unemployment declining at a 1.52% CAGR (World Bank), easing generational demand pressure. However, consumer confidence remains flat (OECD), signaling cautious discretionary spending sentiment. Inflation projections (IMF) suggest stabilization, mitigating real income erosion risks. While the environment is not cyclically fragile, it lacks the dynamism to support aggressive pricing power. Retailers should focus on margin resilience through operational efficiency rather than premium pricing, with short-term revenue visibility contingent on stable but unexceptional demand conditions.

**3. Income Structure & Luxury Market Depth**
Income distribution trends reveal a narrowing spread between the top 10% and bottom 10% (22.0 pp), with both shares declining slightly (World Bank), indicating modestly improving equity but persistent concentration. GDP per capita, when paired with this distribution, suggests a market with limited broad-based aspirational purchasing power but pockets of premium-segment resilience. The income architecture favors a polarized luxury niche over deep premium capture or mid-market breadth. Retailers should position assortments to target high-end segments while avoiding overinvestment in mid-tier categories, ensuring long-term brand scaling aligns with structural demand polarization.
