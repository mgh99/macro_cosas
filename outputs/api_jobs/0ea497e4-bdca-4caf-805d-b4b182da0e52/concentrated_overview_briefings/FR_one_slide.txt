**Executive Market Diagnostic: Apparel & Luxury Sector**

The market exhibits moderate purchasing power dynamics, with GDP growth stabilizing post-pandemic but constrained by inflationary pressures and labor market volatility. Tourism monetization remains a critical growth lever, with high expenditure per arrival (e.g., €859M in 2024) and strong inbound demand from Europe, though dependency on external demand introduces cyclical risks. Demographically, the population is aging, with core working-age cohorts (25–54) plateauing, while net migration and household size trends (2.25 persons/household) suggest limited long-term consumer expansion. Structurally, the market is **premium-concentrated but cyclically exposed**, with tourism-driven demand offsetting domestic purchasing power constraints. Strategic positioning should prioritize high-value tourism monetization while hedging against demographic and external demand volatility.
