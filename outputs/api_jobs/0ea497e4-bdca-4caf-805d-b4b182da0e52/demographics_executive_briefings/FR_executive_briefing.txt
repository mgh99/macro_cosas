**1. Structural Consumer Base & Demographic Momentum**
The market exhibits a **structurally declining demographic fundamentals** trajectory, with total population growth decelerating from 0.5% CAGR (2016–2023) to near-stagnation by 2030. Natural population change has collapsed (2020–2026), offset only by persistent but volatile net migration (90k–180k annually), indicating **migration dependency** for any growth. The core consumption cohort (25–54) is shrinking as a share of the population, while the 65+ cohort expands, accelerating ageing dynamics. The working-age population (15–64) is projected to plateau, constraining labor availability and demand resilience. Forward projections confirm **structural contraction** of the prime consumer base, necessitating strategic retrenchment in expansion plans and heightened focus on high-value, ageing-centric segments.

**2. Household Structure & Consumption Architecture**
Household size has contracted from 2.25 (2011) to 2.22 (2015), signaling a shift toward smaller, individualized consumption units. Combined with ageing demographics, this implies a structural rebalancing toward **premium, discretionary spending** over mass-market demand. The erosion of family-driven household structures suggests declining broad-based consumption capacity, while urbanization and ageing may concentrate demand in niche, high-margin segments. Retail strategies should prioritize **smaller-format, experiential stores** and curated luxury assortments, while deprioritizing labor-intensive, volume-driven models. Long-term brand penetration will hinge on adapting to a **narrowing, high-value consumer base**.
