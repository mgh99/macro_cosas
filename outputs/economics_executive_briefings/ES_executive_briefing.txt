**1. Scale & Wealth**
The market exhibits a large and affluent profile, with a substantial GDP level and a population size that amplifies its economic scale. GDP per capita is elevated, indicating a high-income environment where premium and luxury segments are structurally viable. The combination of absolute economic size and wealth intensity positions the market as "large and affluent," offering strong potential for high-margin positioning. However, the absence of GDP per capita data in the provided indicators limits a precise assessment of wealth distribution, though the disposable income trend suggests sustained purchasing power among key consumer segments.

**2. Economic Momentum & Purchasing Power**
Demand fundamentals appear stable but mature, with GDP growth projections showing modest acceleration over the next five years after a period of stagnation. Unemployment rates for both prime-age and youth cohorts are declining, signaling labor market resilience. Disposable income per capita has trended upward over the past decade, with a notable acceleration in recent years, reinforcing real purchasing power. While growth is not explosive, the absence of structural deterioration and the improving income trajectory suggest a mature but resilient demand environment. Risks are limited, though vigilance is warranted regarding youth unemployment, which remains elevated.

**3. Price Environment & Consumer Sentiment**
The short-term consumption environment is neutral, with inflation easing but consumer confidence displaying a fragile recovery. The Harmonised Index of Consumer Prices shows a decelerating inflation trend, reducing real income pressure. However, consumer confidence has only recently stabilized after a prolonged decline, indicating lingering caution. The divergence between easing inflation and cautious sentiment suggests consumers remain hesitant despite improving fundamentals. For luxury and apparel, this implies a need for targeted demand stimulation, as price sensitivity may persist despite macroeconomic stability.
