**Dynamics & Growth Structure**
The consumer base is structurally stable but ageing, with the 25–49 core consumption segment declining from 31.7% in 2016 to 30.3% in 2025, while the 50+ cohort expands, particularly the 65–79 group, which rises from 13.0% to 15.7% over the same period. Natural population change has weakened significantly, dropping from 190,320 in 2016 to just 17,336 in 2024, indicating a shrinking birth rate. However, net migration has compensated for this decline, averaging over 100,000 annually since 2018, mitigating outright contraction. Projections to 2050 show gradual population growth, but the ageing trend persists, with the 25–49 group likely to further shrink as a share of the total. This suggests long-term demand sustainability will hinge on older cohorts' spending power, while workforce dynamics may face pressure from a shrinking prime-age population. The structural outlook is one of stability with pronounced ageing, requiring strategic adaptation to mature consumer preferences.

**Socio-Economic & Household Structure**
The dominant consumer profile is highly educated and increasingly individual-driven, with upper secondary education attainment rising from 78.1% in 2016 to 83.9% in 2024, signaling higher income potential and consumption sophistication. Average household size has remained stable at 2.1–2.2 members, indicating smaller, more concentrated households. This trend favors individualized spending over family-oriented consumption, particularly in apparel and luxury segments where discretionary purchasing power is concentrated among educated, dual-income households. The interaction of rising education levels and stable household sizes suggests a market where premiumization and personalized experiences will drive demand, though income polarization among older cohorts may create tiered consumption patterns. The structural profile is thus defined by highly educated, smaller-household, individual-driven consumers, with long-term implications for targeted marketing and product differentiation.
