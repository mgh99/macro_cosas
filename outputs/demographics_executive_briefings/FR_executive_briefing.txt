**1. Dynamics & Growth Structure (Indicators 1 + 2 + 5): Is the consumer base expanding, stable, or structurally declining?**

The consumer base is structurally stable but ageing, with a gradual decline in the core 25–49 consumption segment (shrinking from 31.7% in 2016 to 30.3% in 2025) offset by sustained migration inflows. Natural population growth has weakened significantly, dropping from 190,320 in 2016 to just 17,336 in 2024, while net migration has fluctuated but remained positive, compensating for demographic decline. The 50+ cohort is expanding, particularly the 65–79 age group (rising from 13.0% to 15.7%), signaling an ageing workforce and consumer base. Projections indicate modest population growth through 2050, but the structural shift toward older cohorts will pressure long-term demand sustainability, particularly in apparel and luxury segments reliant on younger, higher-spending demographics. Workforce implications include potential labor shortages in growth-oriented sectors, necessitating strategic adaptation to an ageing consumer profile.

**2. Socio-Economic & Household Structure (Indicators 3 + 4): What type of consumers structurally dominate the market?**

The dominant consumer profile is highly educated, with upper secondary attainment rising steadily from 78.1% in 2016 to 83.9% in 2024, reflecting a skilled, income-resilient base. However, average household size has remained stable at 2.2, with a slight dip to 2.1 in 2019 and 2024, suggesting a trend toward smaller, individual-driven households. This implies a structural shift toward individualized spending rather than family-oriented consumption, aligning with luxury and premium apparel demand. The combination of high education levels and smaller households points to a market where sophistication and disposable income are key drivers, though ageing dynamics may polarize income distribution over time. The absence of significant household size contraction mitigates extreme individualization trends, but the overall profile leans toward a highly educated, smaller-household consumer base with refined purchasing preferences.
